The Challenge

Cities worldwide are increasingly turning to AI-powered solutions to tackle the issue of traffic congestion. This problem disrupts the smooth flow of transportation and poses a significant barrier to economic growth.

The Task: Design a system to predict passenger travel demand across various geographic locations at 15-minute intervals using historical spatial-temporal datasets.

Technical Approach

📍

Spatial Decoding

Decoded Base32 Geohashes into numerical Latitude and Longitude coordinates to map accurate spatial proximity.

⏱️

Temporal Features

Utilized sine and cosine transformations for continuous cyclical temporal tracking of hours and minutes.

🎯

Target Encoding

Applied K-Fold target encoding to safely extract signals from high-cardinality categorical constraints.

🧠

Ensemble Modeling

Combined the power of LightGBM, XGBoost, and CatBoost in a robust multi-seed blending architecture.

Key Insight: The "Day 49" Temporal Anomaly

A deep analytical dive into the dataset distributions revealed a severe temporal shift:

  • Training Data (Day 49): Contained only night-time records (0:00 to 2:00).
  • Testing Data (Day 49): Contained only day-time records (2:15 to 23:45).
  • The Shift: Baseline traffic demand at night on Day 49 was actually 55% higher than Day 48.

By identifying this anomaly, the model pipeline was iteratively adjusted (V1 through V6) utilizing log-transformations, sample-weighting, and selective day-dropping to prevent the tree-models from learning a skewed "night-time" bias for recent data.
